# Day 5 — Hai Van Pass Adventure & Farewell Dinner
**Date:** 2026-11-23 (Monday)
**Vibe:** The one early morning of the trip — Vietnam's most iconic coastal road, then a proper farewell dinner and a final night out

---

## Morning
**7:30 AM** — Wake up, coffee, quick breakfast

The one morning with a real alarm. Get everyone fed and ready — resort breakfast or a quick room service order. Cà phê sữa đá (Vietnamese iced coffee) is the morning medicine. Eat something — the motorbike tour is 6–8 hours and you need fuel.

**8:30 AM** — Hai Van Pass Motorbike Tour departs

**What it is:** The Hai Van Pass (Đèo Hải Vân) is a 21 km mountain pass along Vietnam's most dramatic coastal road — featured in Top Gear Season 12 (Clarkson, Hammond, May rode this pass) and widely considered one of the most scenic drives in Southeast Asia. The road climbs from sea level to 496m, with panoramic views of Da Nang Bay, Lang Co Lagoon, and the South China Sea on both sides.

**Tour format for 9 people:**
- **Easy Rider style (recommended):** Each person rides as a passenger on a local experienced guide's motorbike. No motorbike license needed. 9 passengers + 9 guides. Guides are typically local men who know the road, the stops, and the history. Safe, social, and genuinely enjoyable.
- **Self-guided rental (alternative):** Rent 5 semi-automatic bikes, ride with pillions. Only recommended if multiple guys have motorbike experience.

**Booking:** Hai Van Pass motorbike tours are easy to find — search "Hai Van Pass Easy Rider tour Da Nang" on Klook, GetYourGuide, or through hotel concierge. Many operators accommodate groups of 9; confirm capacity. Cost: **~$60–75 per person** for a full-day guided tour with pick-up at hotel.

**8:30 AM pickup** — guides collect the group from the resort. Standard pickup time for full-day tours.

## Afternoon
**The Route (approximately 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM):**

**9:15 AM** — **Marble Mountains stop** (~30 min)
Most full-day Hai Van tours include a Marble Mountains stop on the way out. Five limestone hills with Buddhist caves and shrines, panoramic views of Non Nuoc Beach. Entry ~$2. Quick, worthwhile, on the route.

**10:00 AM** — Ascending Hai Van Pass

The road winds up through jungle and cloud. Stops along the way:
- **Old French and American fortifications** at the summit — war-era bunkers with bullet holes; surreal backdrop for photos
- **Lang Co Beach** — sweeping view of the lagoon and peninsula from the summit
- **Summit tea and coffee stops** — roadside vendors at the top sell Vietnamese iced coffee and snacks. Required stop.

**12:00 PM** — **Lang Co Beach lunch**

The descent from the pass brings the group to Lang Co — a long sandbar peninsula with a lagoon on one side and the open sea on the other. Lunch at a local seafood restaurant here: grilled fish, prawns, rice. Lang Co seafood is remarkably fresh and cheap (~$10–15 per person). Guides will recommend a local spot.

**2:00 PM** — **Mo Waterfall / return route exploration**

Some operators include a detour to Mo Waterfall or through scenic inland routes on the return. Ask the operator what they include — the route flexibility is one of the Easy Rider format's strengths.

**4:00–4:30 PM** — Return to resort

Drop-off at the resort. The group should be back by 4:30 PM with enough time to shower, rest, and get ready for the farewell dinner.

## Evening
**6:00 PM** — Pool debrief & photo sharing

Back at the villas. Cold beers by the pool. Everyone shares Hai Van Pass photos — this will be the adventure photo highlight of the trip. 30–45 minutes of genuine debrief before getting ready.

**7:30 PM** — Farewell Dinner at La Maison 1888

**Location:** InterContinental Danang Sun Peninsula Resort (or confirm current location — La Maison 1888 has historically been Da Nang's top fine dining venue, MICHELIN Guide Vietnam 2025 honoree)
**Cuisine:** European-standard fine dining; multi-course; exceptional wine list

This is the formal farewell dinner for the group — the occasion to mark the end of the trip before the final night. For 9 people at a MICHELIN-recognized restaurant, the reservation needs to be made **at least 2–4 weeks in advance**. Specify "bachelor party group of 9, special occasion" when booking.

Budget: **~$80–120 per person** with wine and cocktails. This is the premium meal of the trip — worth every dong.

If La Maison 1888 can't accommodate 9 or timing doesn't work, the fallback is **My Hanh Seafood** (used on Day 1 — book a second visit; the group will want to return) or a private seafood feast at a beach restaurant (Ca Chuon Co offers private dining for parties).

## Night
**10:30 PM** — Final night out: Hoi An-style or Koto Club

Two options for the final night, depending on group energy:

**Option A (Energetic): Koto Club** (254 Vo Nguyen Giap Street, An Hai Ward; open 24/7)
The beach-adjacent club with weekly themed nights — Sexy Dance, Belly Dance, Carnival, China Dance. No pre-booking needed (or call ahead for a VIP table). EDM; good production. Located on the beach strip near the resort — 10 min Grab. A solid final-night club that doesn't require the full VIP table commitment of ADM or New Oriental.

**Option B (Relaxed): Hoi An evening trip**
If the group prefers a different vibe for the last night, Hoi An at night is genuinely magical — 45 min south by private van. Lantern-lit ancient streets, cocktail bars along the Thu Bon River, riverside restaurants. Private van round-trip: ~$50–80 for the group. Less of a club night, more of a memorable visual experience. The town's bar scene on Monday night is relaxed, which fits a group that's already had two massive club nights.

The default recommendation for a bachelor party that has already hit ADM and New Oriental is **Option A: Koto Club** — keep the nightlife momentum for one more night before the departure day hangs over everything.

**~2:00 AM** — Back to the villas. Last pool beers. Pack bags or at least acknowledge that packing exists.

---

## Logistics
- Hai Van Pass tour operators pick up from the hotel — no transport coordination needed for the morning. Confirm pickup time and meeting point with the operator 24 hours before.
- The full-day tour includes motorbikes and guides — no additional transport needed until the evening.
- Grab from resort to La Maison 1888: confirm the exact address when booking, as the InterContinental is on the Son Tra Peninsula (~20 min from main resort area, $8–12).
- For Koto Club: Grab from resort is ~10 min, $4–6. Koto is on Vo Nguyen Giap, same strip as the beach clubs.
- If choosing Hoi An Option B: book a 16-seat private van through the hotel concierge. Depart ~7 PM (after dinner), explore Hoi An 8–11 PM, return by midnight.

## Notes & Tips
- **8:30 AM pickup is real.** Set alarms the night before. Brief the group on Sunday. The Hai Van Pass is the adventure centerpiece of the trip — don't miss it because three guys overslept.
- Wear comfortable clothes for the motorbike tour — shorts and a t-shirt are fine. Guides provide helmets. No need for full motorbike gear; it's not a high-speed tour.
- Sunscreen for the Hai Van Pass: the road at altitude has strong UV exposure. Apply before departure.
- La Maison 1888 reservation: make this before the trip. It's the hardest table in Da Nang to walk into as a group of 9.
- Start loose packing on Day 5 evening — checkout on Day 6 comes faster than expected.
- The final villa pool session after the club is a tradition worth keeping. Last beers, stories from the week, someone will inevitably jump in fully clothed.
