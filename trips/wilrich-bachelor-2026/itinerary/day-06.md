# Day 6 — Checkout, Final Brunch & Departure
**Date:** 2026-11-24 (Tuesday)
**Vibe:** A slow, grateful morning — final Vietnamese iced coffee, pack up, and send everyone home right

---

## Morning
**[No early alarm unless flights demand it]** — Final morning at the villa

The last morning. Depending on flight times, there's no reason to rush. Check-out at most Da Nang resorts is 12 PM (noon) — some properties allow late checkout until 2 PM for a fee; for a group staying in multiple villas, ask the hotel for a complimentary 1 PM checkout given the room volume. This is worth requesting.

Pack bags. Do a room sweep for forgotten items (chargers behind headboards, sunglasses by the pool, dress shoes under beds after club nights). Do a group luggage check at the villa before heading to the lobby.

**9:00 AM** — Final brunch at the resort beach restaurant

The last group meal. Order big — Vietnamese breakfast spread with Mì Quảng (Da Nang's turmeric yellow noodle dish), bánh mì, fresh fruit plates, and multiple rounds of cà phê sữa đá. The resort's beach restaurant should do a full brunch service. Sit at the beachfront tables if the weather allows.

This is also the moment to settle any outstanding resort charges, confirm any group bar tabs from the pool days, and sort any cash that needs to be squared between people.

**10:30 AM – 11:30 AM** — Final pool session / beach walk

For those with afternoon flights, there's time for one last pool float or a walk down to My Khe Beach. Even 30 minutes by the water on the final morning is worth it. Take the group photo nobody took on Day 1.

**11:30 AM** — Check-out

Settle bills, collect luggage, tip the villa staff (tipping is appreciated but not expected in Vietnamese culture; 50,000–100,000 VND per person per night is a good guideline for outstanding service). Group checkout for 9 people can take 30–45 minutes — factor this into departure timing.

## Afternoon
**12:30 PM – 2:00 PM (flexible)** — Da Nang Street Food Farewell Crawl

The last proper meal of the trip — a final tour through the four dishes most specific to Da Nang before the airport. All stops are in the Hai Chau District, within 10–15 minutes of each other. Total spend ~$10–15 per person for all four. If flights are early, prioritize stops 1 and 2.

**Stop 1 — Mì Quảng Bà Mua** (19–21 Truong Thi Street, Hai Chau)
Da Nang's defining dish: turmeric-yellow rice noodles with pork, shrimp, crispy sesame crackers, peanuts, and fresh herbs in a small amount of rich concentrated broth. Not pho — drier, more complex, unmistakably Da Nang. Under $2 per bowl.

**Stop 2 — Bánh Xèo Bà Dưỡng** (280/23 Hoang Dieu Street, Hai Chau)
Vietnam's most famous sizzling pancakes — Anthony Bourdain ate here. Crispy rice-flour crepes filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts, eaten wrapped in rice paper with fresh herbs and dipped in fish sauce. ~$3–5 per person. The Da Nang version is drier and crispier than the southern style.

**Stop 3 — Bánh Tráng Cuốn Thịt Heo** (street stalls near Tran Phu Street)
Da Nang's most local specialty — thinly sliced pork with crispy shallots and herbs, hand-rolled at the table in rice paper, dipped in mắm nêm (fermented shrimp sauce). Polarizing on the sauce, unforgettable regardless. Only found in Da Nang. ~$3–5 per person.

**Stop 4 — Bún Chả Cá** (any local spot, Nguyen Thi Minh Khai Street area)
Da Nang's fishermen's noodle soup — fish cakes in a light, clean broth with rice vermicelli. Eaten for breakfast by locals but available all day. The lightest dish of the crawl; good final bite before a flight. ~$2 per bowl.

If flights are tight, skip to the airport — Da Nang airport food is functional but not a priority.

**[Flight-dependent]** — Private minivan to Da Nang International Airport (DAD)

Pre-arrange the airport transfer with the hotel concierge or the same operator used for Day 1 arrival. For 9 people with full luggage, a single 16-seat minivan is the cleanest option (~$25–35 for the group, $3–4 per person). Confirm the van booking the night before — don't leave this to the morning of.

Da Nang International Airport is small. Domestic check-in closes 45 minutes before departure; international check-in closes 60 minutes before. Allow 30–45 minutes at the airport before those windows.

International connections typically go via Singapore (SIN), Kuala Lumpur (KUL), Bangkok (BKK), or Ho Chi Minh City (SGN) for onward flights.

## Logistics
- Hotel checkout: 12 PM standard; ask reception for late checkout (1–2 PM) the evening before. Offer to pay the late fee if needed — worth it for the group.
- Airport minivan: Pre-book the night before with hotel concierge or the Day 1 operator. Confirm pickup time based on flight departure times.
- Da Nang airport (DAD) is approximately 15–20 km from the main resort area — allow 25–40 min drive depending on traffic.
- For groups with different flight times: those departing earlier can Grab to the airport while others finish brunch. Coordinate the night before.

## Notes & Tips
- Pack on the night of Day 5 (or at least stage bags) — morning checkouts are always less chaotic when bags are ready the night before.
- Tip villa housekeeping staff before checkout: 50,000–100,000 VND per night per room is appropriate for exceptional service.
- Exchange any remaining large VND notes at the airport or at the hotel front desk before departure — banks and airports abroad rarely take VND.
- Do a group photo before anyone leaves for the airport. Someone always forgets.
- If anyone has leftover VND (Vietnamese dong), it can be exchanged at the airport or held for a return trip. The bills are worth keeping as souvenirs regardless.
- Bring snacks or eat before the airport — Da Nang airport food is functional but not memorable.
