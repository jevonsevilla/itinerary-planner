# Day 4 — Full Recovery & Rooftop Sunday
**Date:** 2026-11-22 (Sunday)
**Vibe:** Two heavy nights in a row earns a genuine rest day — pool, beach, good food, and a sunset rooftop to cap it off

---

## Morning
**[No alarm]** — Sleep in, villa recovery

After back-to-back big nights (ADM Friday, New Oriental Saturday), Sunday is a recovery day. No activities before noon. No exceptions. The villa pool is the destination for most of the morning — sunscreen, cold water, coffee from room service, and very little else.

Encourage everyone to eat something at the resort restaurant between 11 AM and 1 PM. The Hyatt Regency and Premier Village both have solid Sunday lunch options including fresh seafood and Vietnamese staples. Eat slowly. Drink water.

## Afternoon
**12:00 PM** — Optional: Vietnamese Massage & Spa

Before heading out, anyone who wants a proper Vietnamese massage has time for one now. A traditional full-body massage runs 60–90 min and costs $10–25 at reputable local spas near the resort. Ask the hotel concierge for their top recommendation — most luxury resorts in the area have in-house spas (Amiana Spa at Hyatt, Premier Village Spa). Go for the full treatment: aromatherapy, hot stone, the works. This is one of Vietnam's great luxuries, and at Da Nang prices it's essentially free.

**1:00 PM** — Hoi An Half-Day Trip & Food Crawl

Hoi An is 45 km south of Da Nang — Vietnam's most iconic ancient town and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. One of the most photographed places in Southeast Asia, and the country's undisputed street food capital. The recovery-day afternoon is ideal timing: late enough for the group to be functional, and back in time for sunset drinks.

**Transport:** Pre-book a private 16-seat minivan round-trip through the hotel concierge or Klook (~$60–80 for the group / ~$7–9 per person). Allow 45–60 min each way.

**The Hoi An Food Crawl (2:00 PM – 4:30 PM):**

**Stop 1 — Cao Lầu (2:00 PM)**
**Where:** Thanh Cao Lau, 26 Thai Phien Street (or street stalls near Hoi An Central Market)
Cao Lầu is a Hoi An-exclusive dish — thick, chewy rice noodles made with local well water (the recipe can't be replicated elsewhere), served with pork slices, crispy croutons, fresh greens, and a small amount of broth. This dish exists only in Hoi An. ~$2–3 per bowl.

**Stop 2 — White Rose Dumplings (2:30 PM)**
**Where:** White Rose Restaurant, 533 Hai Ba Trung Street
Translucent steamed shrimp dumplings shaped like roses — another Hoi An exclusive. Only one family in the world makes the wrappers and supplies every restaurant in town. Delicate, fresh, and impossible to find elsewhere. ~$3–5 per order.

**Stop 3 — Bánh Mì Phượng (3:00 PM)**
**Where:** 2B Phan Chu Trinh Street
Anthony Bourdain called it "the most amazing, delicious, inexpensive sandwich in the world." Crispy baguette, pork pâté, cold cuts, pickled daikon and carrot, fresh herbs, and chili. The queue moves fast. ~$1.50 each — get two.

**Stop 4 — Chè / Vietnamese Dessert (3:20 PM)**
Any roadside stall on the Ancient Town streets. Sweet bean soups, coconut jelly, and shaved ice — Vietnam's street dessert. Cold, sweet, refreshing. ~$1 per cup.

**3:30 PM — Japanese Covered Bridge & Ancient Town**
Wander the lantern-lit lanes of the UNESCO Heritage Zone. The Japanese Covered Bridge (Chùa Cầu) is Hoi An's most iconic landmark — 400 years old, surrounded by old merchant houses. Allow 30–45 min to walk the area and take the obligatory yellow-wall alley photos.

**Optional add-on: Lantern Making Class (~$15–20 pp, 90 min)**
Hoi An is the lantern capital of Vietnam. A hands-on workshop to make your own silk lantern is the quintessential Hoi An experience. Book in advance through the hotel or Klook (Rose Lanterns and Hoi An Lanterns Workshop are both highly rated). Schedule at 2:00 PM and shift the food crawl to 3:30 PM if adding this.

**Optional add-on: Half-Day Cooking Class (~$35–50 pp, 3 hours)**
Hoi An is home to some of Vietnam's best cooking schools (Red Bridge, Morning Glory, Hoi An Cooking Class by Hai). A half-day class includes a local market tour, hands-on cooking of 4–5 dishes, and eating everything you make. The best cultural Vietnam experience on the whole trip. If adding this, depart Da Nang at noon instead of 1 PM.

**4:30 PM** — Depart Hoi An, back to Da Nang by 5:30 PM.

## Evening
**6:30 PM** — Golden hour at Sky36 Rooftop Bar

**Address:** 36th floor, Novotel Danang Premier Han River, 36 Bach Dang Street, Hai Chau District
**Hours:** ~6 PM – 2 AM (confirm directly at sky36.vn)
**Dress code:** Smart casual mandatory — no shorts, no sandals, no sleeveless shirts for men. This is the one venue that enforces it. Have everyone in long trousers and closed shoes.

Sky36 is the highest rooftop bar in Vietnam, with 360-degree views of the Dragon Bridge, Han River, city skyline, and mountains. The sunset from up here, with cocktails, is genuinely one of the best experiences Da Nang offers — and it's a significant change of pace from the club energy of the previous two nights.

**Drinks pricing:**
- Cocktails: ~400,000 VND (~$16)
- Beer: ~120,000 VND (~$5)
- After 10 PM: bottle service only (no soft drinks or mocktails). Arrive at 6:30–7 PM to maximize cocktail time.

**VIP table:** ~2,300,000 VND (~$100) per VIP table near the stage, includes a whiskey bottle + mixers + meat platter (for 2; for 9 people, book 4–5 VIP tables or negotiate a group package directly). This is a lounge/bar experience, not a dance club — the vibe is more sophisticated and relaxed.

Book in advance, especially for a group of 9 on a Sunday evening. Call or book via sky36.vn.

**9:00 PM** — Dinner at The Temptation

**Location:** Overlooking the Han River (city center)
Stunning river views, European-Vietnamese fusion menu, quality cocktail list. Perfect for a Sunday group dinner after the rooftop — quieter than a Friday/Saturday booking, good service for large groups. Budget ~$40–60 per person with drinks.

Alternatively, post-Sky36 the group can walk along Bach Dang Street and choose from the cluster of restaurants along the riverfront — the area has multiple solid options without a reservation.

## Night
**10:00 PM – Midnight (optional)** — Chill bar-hop, An Thuong area

Sunday night is not a club night. But if the group has energy after dinner, the An Thuong bar strip is the right scale — casual open-fronted bars, no VIP table minimums, and walkable.

Good spots for a Sunday wind-down:
- **The Mad Den** (Irish pub; welcoming to groups; pool table)
- **Minsk Bar** (reggae vibes; cold beers; relaxed)
- **Simple Man Pub / Crafty Corner** (open-fronted; live music some nights)

Cut the night by midnight. **Day 5 is the big adventure day** (Hai Van Pass motorbike tour) with a pickup at 8:30 AM. This is the one early start of the trip.

## Logistics
- Hoi An is 45 km south of Da Nang (~45–60 min drive). Pre-book a 16-seat private minivan through the hotel concierge or Klook — book the day before, not same-day.
- Hoi An Ancient Town is largely car-free — the group walks once inside. Comfortable shoes recommended; some streets are cobbled.
- Depart Da Nang at 1:00 PM → arrive Hoi An ~2:00 PM → depart Hoi An at 4:30 PM → back at resort ~5:30 PM → freshen up → Grab to Sky36 at 6:30 PM.
- Sky36 is on Bach Dang Street, Han River — same area as OQ Lounge and within walking distance of several dinner options. Grab from resort: ~15 min, $5–8.
- Remind the group on Sunday evening: **Hai Van Pass tour pickup is 8:30 AM Monday morning**. Set an alarm. This is non-negotiable — the guide/operator waits for the group.

## Notes & Tips
- Hoi An food crawl booking: Cao Lau and White Rose are walk-in; Bánh Mì Phượng is always queue-and-go. No reservations needed for the crawl.
- If adding a cooking class or lantern workshop to Hoi An, book these in advance through the hotel concierge or Klook and depart Da Nang at noon instead of 1 PM.
- Sky36 dress code is enforced. Brief the group before leaving the resort — anyone in shorts and sandals will be turned away. Check before getting in the Grab.
- The rooftop can be windy in November. Light jackets or a shirt layer is worth bringing. Evenings at 23°C feel cooler with wind.
- Sunday recovery day is intentional — two heavy club nights followed by a genuine rest day keeps the group functional for the adventure on Day 5.
- The Han River area (Bach Dang Street) is walkable for dinner after Sky36 — explore the riverfront strip.
- Remind everyone to charge phones and get a solid sleep tonight. Hai Van Pass requires an early alarm.
