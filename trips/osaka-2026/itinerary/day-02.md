## Notes

- **Book ahead**: Reserve Pontocho dinner (Hachidaime Gihey and Robin both book up on Saturdays) and **Bar Kohaku at Park Hyatt Kyoto** (small room, Saturday golden-hour window fills). Everything else walk-in.
- **Cherry blossoms**: By April 18, most Kyoto blossoms have finished — but **Philosopher's Path** and **Maruyama Park** (weeping cherry) often have late varieties still clinging on. The path itself is beautiful regardless, with the canal and stone bridges.
- **Skippable**: Honen-in (halfway on the Philosopher's Path) — a hidden moss-gate temple, free and 10 min tops. Skip only if you're chasing daylight for Kiyomizu-dera.
- **Tips**:
  - **Arrive Fushimi Inari by 08:30** — 09:00+ brings tour bus crowds; Saturday is the worst day of the week
  - Yotsutsuji Intersection viewpoint is the smart turnaround — full summit loop is 2 hr and the view stops getting better
  - Fushimi sake district is **right next to Fushimi Inari** — combining them avoids a separate trip later in the week
  - **Philosopher's Path flows north-to-south**: start at Ginkaku-ji, end at Nanzen-ji, then bus south to Kiyomizu — the geography works out perfectly
  - Kiyomizu-dera closes at 18:00 — don't dawdle at Nanzen-ji
  - Sannen-zaka / Ninen-zaka are covered Edo-era slopes with matcha ice cream, pickle shops, and rickshaws — the postcard Kyoto walk
  - Gion geisha spotting most likely 18:00–19:00 on Hanamikoji Street
  - **Saturday is the biggest nightlife night of the week in Kyoto** — but Kyoto's pace is different from Osaka's; lean into the slower rhythm (nice izakaya + 1–2 bars), not clubbing
- **Estimated Day Cost**: ¥14,000–24,000 (transport ¥880 + attractions ¥1,800 + food ¥5,000–8,500 + drinks ¥5,000–8,000 + Kyoto hotel ¥8,000–15,000)
