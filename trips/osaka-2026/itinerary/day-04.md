## Notes

- Cherry blossoms: **This is THE cherry blossom highlight of the trip!** Yoshino has 30,000 trees blooming base-to-summit over weeks. By April 20, Shimo-Senbon (lower) will be finished, but **Kami-Senbon and Oku-Senbon bloom mid-to-late April** — perfect timing. Hanayagura Viewpoint offers the best panoramic view of thousands of cherry trees.
- Booking required: Kintetsu Limited Express reserved seats recommended (purchase at station or online)
- Weather backup: Cherry blossoms visible in light rain; bring umbrella and waterproof shoes. Shorten hike to Naka-Senbon if heavy rain.
- Tips:
  - **Monday = off-peak weekday** — far fewer crowds than weekends (Yoshino gets mobbed on weekends during cherry season)
  - 4 areas from bottom to top: Shimo-Senbon → Naka-Senbon → Kami-Senbon → Oku-Senbon
  - Kami-Senbon has the best views; Oku-Senbon has the latest blooms
  - Kintetsu is NOT covered by JR Pass (private railway)
  - Wear hiking shoes; ~400m total elevation gain over 3-4 hours
  - Bring water, snacks, charged phone
  - Kinpusen-ji Temple (Naka-Senbon) is a large wooden temple worth stopping at
  - Total walking: 8-12km — pace yourself
  - Kakinoha-zushi and kudzu (arrowroot) dishes are Yoshino specialties

**Estimated Day Cost**: ¥10,000-14,000 (transport ¥2,960-3,190 + food ¥4,100-6,800 + drinks ¥2,000-3,000)
