## Notes

- **Book ahead**: **Fukujuen Uji Kobo** — book the matcha-making slot online. **Shigetsu at Tenryu-ji** — reserve for lunch. **Gion Karyo** or any kaiseki dinner — reserve. Taihoan and Nakamura Tokichi are walk-in.
- **Cherry blossoms**: Kyoto lowland blooms mostly finished by April 19. Arashiyama is beautiful regardless — the bamboo, river, and mountain views are the draw.
- **Skippable**: Tenryu-ji — swap for Monkey Park Iwatayama (¥600, hike to 170 wild monkeys with panoramic views), a Hozugawa River boat ride (¥4,100, book ahead), or a rickshaw ride (¥4,000–13,000).
- **Tips**:
  - **Arrive Arashiyama by 08:45** to beat tour bus crowds in the bamboo grove (Sunday is busy but not quite Saturday-level)
  - Kinkakuji is beautiful but compact — 1 hour is enough; it's a one-way path
  - Fukujuen Uji Kobo: the matcha-grinding experience with a traditional stone mill is the classic (¥1,650)
  - **Uji is the matcha capital of Japan** — bring extra yen for high-quality matcha souvenirs
  - Byodo-in closes 17:30 — don't skip it, the Phoenix Hall is the whole point of Uji's UNESCO listing
  - **Last night in Kyoto** — pack bags before bed; Day 4 has an early-ish morning transfer to Osaka before the Yoshino run
- **Estimated Day Cost**: ¥13,000–22,000 (transport ¥1,640 + attractions ¥2,600–3,000 + matcha workshop ¥1,650 + food ¥5,000–11,500 + drinks ¥2,500–4,000 + Kyoto hotel ¥8,000–15,000)
