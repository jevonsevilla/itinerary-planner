## Weather & When to Visit

**Late June–early July** is peak summer on the French Riviera — ideal for the trip's purpose.

| Metric | June | July |
|--------|------|------|
| Average High | 26°C (79°F) | 29°C (84°F) |
| Average Low | 18°C (64°F) | 21°C (70°F) |
| Rainfall | Low (~30 mm) | Very low (~15 mm) |
| Sea Temp | ~23°C | ~25°C |
| Sunshine Hours | ~10 hrs/day | ~11 hrs/day |

- Expect hot, sunny days with very little rain — classic Côte d'Azur summer
- Evenings are warm and pleasant; a light layer is useful for dinner terraces
- The Croisette and beaches get very crowded from July 1 onward; June 25–30 is still manageable

**What to pack:**
- Lightweight clothing (linen, cotton)
- Swimwear — multiple sets
- Comfortable walking sandals and one pair of smart shoes for evening
- Sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses, hat — the Mediterranean sun is intense
- Smart casual / semi-formal outfits for clubs and upscale restaurants
- A light cardigan for air-conditioned interiors and cooler ferry rides
- Proposal/prenup outfits (suggest a change of clothes for different looks)

## Entry Requirements

- **Philippines → France / Schengen:** Filipino citizens currently require a **Schengen Visa** to enter France (and all EU/Schengen countries). Apply at the French Embassy/VFS Global in Manila at least 4–6 weeks before departure.
- **Passport validity:** Must be valid for at least 3 months beyond the planned departure from the Schengen Area
- **Schengen stay:** Maximum 90 days within any 180-day rolling period across the entire Schengen Zone
- **Proof of funds:** Approx. €100/day per person is commonly cited; bank statements or credit card evidence required
- **Travel insurance:** Mandatory for Schengen visa application; must cover at least €30,000 in medical coverage
- **Vaccinations:** No specific requirements for France, but standard travel vaccines (Hep A/B, tetanus) are recommended; COVID-19 vaccination is no longer required
- **ETIAS:** The EU's travel authorization scheme (ETIAS) has been announced but check its status closer to the travel date, as rollout has been delayed multiple times

## Safety & Health

**General safety:** Cannes is a safe city, especially in tourist areas. As a glamorous resort town, it is heavily policed during major events.

**Petty crime to watch for:**
- Pickpockets operate on La Croisette, in Marché Forville, and around the train station — use a money belt or cross-body bags
- Scam artists and unofficial "guides" near the Palais des Festivals, especially during events
- During Cannes Lions festival week, the city is flooded with industry professionals and parties; stay aware in crowds

**Areas that are generally fine at night:** Croisette, Le Suquet, Rue des Frères Pradignac (bar district), Old Port area

**Health:**
- Tap water is safe to drink throughout France
- Pharmacies (marked with a green cross) are everywhere and well-stocked — pharmacists speak basic English
- Sun: the combination of heat reflection off the Mediterranean and high UV index makes sunburn a real risk; reapply sunscreen throughout the day
- No special health requirements or advisories

## Getting Around

**Within Cannes:**
- The city center is very **walkable** — La Croisette, Le Suquet, the Old Port, Rue d'Antibes, and the train station are all within a 15–20 minute walk of each other
- **Local buses (Azur Bus):** Single ticket ~€1.50; useful for reaching Palm Beach or Californie neighborhoods
- **Taxis:** Available at stands near the Croisette, Palais des Festivals, and train station; ~€8–€15 for local rides
- **Uber/Bolt:** Available and often more convenient during the festival
- **Cycling:** Vélo Bleue bike-share stations scattered throughout the city; €1/30 min or day pass available
- **Scooter rental:** Several agencies near the port; useful for exploring the coastal road

**Day trips (from Cannes train station — Gare de Cannes):**
- Antibes: 8–12 min, ~€3 round-trip
- Nice: 28–35 min, ~€8–€12 round-trip
- Monaco: ~75 min (change at Nice), ~€14–€18 round-trip
- Grasse: ~40 min by bus
- Lérins Islands: 15–20 min ferry

## Where to Stay

Cannes has five distinct areas suited to different styles. For this mid-range group, **La Croisette / Center** and **Le Suquet / Old Port** are the best bases.

### 1. La Croisette / Boulevard Central
**Best for:** Maximum convenience during Lions festival, beach access, nightlife proximity
- **Pros:** Walking distance to Palais des Festivals, beach clubs, clubs, restaurants; festival energy
- **Cons:** Most expensive area; crowded and loud during peak season
- **Mid-range hotels:** Hotel Gray d'Albion (central, popular during Lions), Hôtel Cavendish (boutique, good value), Best Western Plus Cannes Riviera
- **Typical nightly rate (mid-range):** €150–€250/night in late June

### 2. Le Suquet / Old Port (Vieux Port)
**Best for:** Charm, authentic local feel, great restaurants, photography
- **Pros:** Beautiful medieval setting, quieter evenings, excellent dining scene, gorgeous views from the hilltop; romantic atmosphere ideal for proposal/prenup
- **Cons:** Some steep streets; 10–15 min walk to main beach areas
- **Mid-range hotels:** Hotel Splendid (seafront, Art Nouveau), Hôtel de France (budget-to-mid), local apartments via Airbnb
- **Typical nightly rate (mid-range):** €120–€200/night

### 3. Cannes Centre / Rue d'Antibes
**Best for:** Practical base, shopping access, mid-range budget
- **Pros:** Most affordable mid-range options, central to everything, near train station
- **Cons:** Less glamorous, heavier street traffic
- **Mid-range hotels:** Hotel Renoir, Hotel Ligure, Novotel Cannes Montfleury
- **Typical nightly rate (mid-range):** €100–€180/night

### 4. Palm Beach / Pointe Croisette (Southeast)
**Best for:** Quieter, more residential feel; access to casino and beach clubs
- **Pros:** Less crowded, great beach, near Palm Beach Casino; beautiful at sunset
- **Cons:** ~30-min walk from festival/center; limited dining options nearby
- **Typical nightly rate (mid-range):** €130–€200/night

> **Recommendation for this group:** Book in the **Croisette / Center** for the Lions festival days (June 25–27), then consider moving to **Le Suquet area** for a more photogenic and relaxed stay. Alternatively, a single apartment or Airbnb near Rue d'Antibes covers both needs efficiently.

## Cannes Lions Festival — Young Lions Competition (Special Focus)

### What Is It?

The **Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity** is the global ad industry's most prestigious event, held annually in Cannes. **2026 dates: June 22–26.**

The **Young Lions Competition** is a sub-event within Cannes Lions open to advertising and marketing professionals **under 30 years old, competing in pairs.** Categories include: Print, Digital, Film, Design, Media, PR, and **Young Marketers (Marketing category)**.

### How the Competition Works

1. Upon arrival at Cannes, the two competing friends receive a **brief from an NGO, charity, or social-good brand**
2. They have **24 hours** to develop a full campaign response
3. The campaign is presented to a **jury of senior global creatives**
4. Winners are announced during the festival week
5. The competition area is located in the **Cannes Lions School Campus** at the Palais des Festivals

**Note for the competitors:** The group arrives June 25 — the festival's second-to-last day. Check with the Young Lions competition administrators **in advance** for the exact schedule — the Marketing category brief and judging timing may affect the first day's plans. In past years the 24-hour brief typically runs mid-week (Tuesday–Wednesday), so arriving on June 25 means the competition work may already be submitted; competitors may be awaiting results or celebrating.

### For Non-Competing Friends During the Festival

While the Palais des Festivals itself requires a badge, non-badge holders have significant access to the festival atmosphere:

**Beach pavilions on La Croisette:**
- Major global companies (Google, Meta, Spotify, Publicis, WPP, etc.) set up beach "pavilions" — branded lounge areas along the beach. Many offer open hours, RSVP-accessible events, free drinks, and networking opportunities.
- **LBB & Friends Beach** at La Plage Croisette is the most accessible to non-badge holders — daily panels, casual meetups; free or RSVP-based
- **Journal House** (61 Bd de la Croisette): open programming with thought leaders
- Check Cannes Lions social media and industry newsletters (LBBOnline, Campaign, Adweek) for 2026 event listings and open RSVP links — most are announced in the weeks before

**Evening parties:**
- Many brand and agency parties during Lions week are RSVP-accessible and not badge-restricted — keep an eye on industry event aggregators like Akommo.com and Tappx.com as June 2026 approaches
- The Croisette area is full of spontaneous energy: pop-up activations, outdoor screenings, themed happy hours at beach clubs

**What to do on La Croisette during festival week:**
- Walk the Croisette and soak in the industry atmosphere — celebrity sightings common
- Beach clubs like Nikki Beach, Baoli Beach, and Vegaluna are ideal for daytime socializing with industry crowds
- Casino Barrière Les Princes is open to the public year-round (ID required, smart dress)

## Proposal & Prenup Photography

Cannes and the broader French Riviera offer some of the world's most scenic and romantic settings. Multiple local photographers specialize specifically in proposals and prenup shoots.

### Top Locations in Cannes

**1. Le Suquet (Old Town) — Hilltop & Alleys**
- The elevated streets, stone staircases, bougainvillea-draped walls, and the clock tower viewpoint overlooking the bay are classically romantic
- The view from the top of Rue du Mont Chevalier at sunset over the Lérins Islands is extraordinary
- Best for: candid romantic portraits in a historic setting; "paparazzi-style" shooting where photographer stays hidden

**2. La Croisette at Sunrise / Golden Hour**
- Before the crowds arrive (6:00–8:00 AM), La Croisette is nearly empty; the Mediterranean glows gold
- The Carlton and Martinez hotel facades provide an elegant backdrop
- Best for: clean, iconic French Riviera shots; sunrise proposal timing

**3. Île Sainte-Marguerite**
- A 15-minute ferry ride from Cannes; the island has secluded coves, turquoise water, pine forests, and historic Fort Royal
- Feels completely removed from the city — private and timeless
- Best for: intimate proposal, barefoot on a deserted cove; lush natural backdrops

**4. Pointe Croisette / Palm Beach Area**
- The southern tip of the Croisette offers dramatic coastal views looking back toward the city
- Less crowded than central Croisette; good for a natural, windswept feel

### Wider French Riviera Options (Better for Prenup)

**5. Eze Village (30 km from Cannes, ~45 min by train + bus)**
- Medieval hilltop perched 427m above sea; panoramic views of Monaco and the coast
- Exotic garden at the summit; narrow stone streets; extremely photogenic
- Best for: dramatic elevated shots; sunrise/sunset magic hour

**6. Cap d'Antibes / Garoupe Beach (20 km, ~25 min by train)**
- Rocky coastline, turquoise coves, lighthouse; elegant and secluded
- Best for: natural/coastal couple portraits; less crowded than Cannes beaches

**7. Saint-Paul-de-Vence (50 km, ~1 hr by car/bus)**
- Fortified hilltop art village; rampart walks with views; Fondation Maeght sculpture garden
- Very romantic, artsy atmosphere; lavender and stone walls

**8. Grasse Lavender Fields & Old Town (25 km, ~40 min)**
- The perfume capital of the world; lavender fields in June/July; charming old town streets
- Lavender fields near Plateau de Valensole are ~1.5 hrs away but worth the trip for a full prenup day

### Photography Logistics

- **Local photographers:** Multiple professionals specialize in French Riviera proposals and prenup sessions. Platforms like LocalLens (locallens.com) list Cannes photographers with packages
- **Rates:** Typically €300–€600 for a 1–2 hour session; €600–€1,200 for a half-day prenup shoot with multiple locations
- **Booking:** Book at least 4–6 weeks in advance; peak summer season fills up quickly
- **Best time of day:** Golden hour (6:30–8:00 AM or 7:30–9:00 PM in late June/July) for the most flattering light
- **Cannes-based specialists:** Search on LocalLens, Cannes Proposals (cannesproposals.com), WildRoses Photography (wildroses.fr) — all specialize in French Riviera romance photography

## Food & Drink

Cannes has an excellent dining scene ranging from Provençal market stalls to Michelin-starred restaurants. For a mid-range group, aim for €25–€60/person per meal.

### Must-Try Dishes & Drinks

- **Socca:** Chickpea pancake baked in a wood-fired oven — the iconic Niçois street food; find it at Marché Forville
- **Salade Niçoise:** The real version uses local tuna, anchovies, boiled egg, and fresh vegetables (no cooked vegetables in the authentic version)
- **Pan Bagnat:** Niçoise salad stuffed in a round bread roll — perfect picnic food
- **Bouillabaisse:** Marseille-origin fish stew; some Cannes restaurants do excellent versions
- **Pissaladière:** Onion tart with anchovies and olives — classic Provençal
- **Rosé wine:** This is Provence — drink local rosé. Bandol and Côtes de Provence AOC are the gold standards. A pichet (carafe) is the economical way to drink it
- **Pastis:** The anise aperitif; ordered with a jug of cold water; locals drink it pre-dinner

### Restaurant Recommendations

#### Fine Dining / Special Occasion (€80–€150+/person)
- **La Palme d'Or** — Hôtel Martinez; Michelin-starred; the quintessential Cannes fine dining experience; book weeks in advance
- **La Môme** — 6 Rue Florian; famous crudo towers, fresh pasta, wagyu; the "see and be seen" spot; great for a birthday dinner or big group celebration; live music on weekends; €60–€120/person without drinks

#### Mid-Range / Great Value (€30–€60/person)
- **Anna Cannes** — 63 Bd de la Croisette; Greek mezze; excellent seafood; beautiful terrace on the Croisette; very popular during Lions week
- **Bobo Bistro** — 21 Rue du Commandant André; French Mediterranean; best pizza in Cannes; relaxed atmosphere; recommended for groups
- **Mido** — 5 Rue Florian; Nikkei (Japanese-Peruvian fusion); tiraditos, ceviche, charcoal-grilled fish; excellent quality for the price
- **Sens** — 9 Rue d'Oran; contemporary European; open kitchen concept; locally sourced ingredients; great for a food-forward dinner
- **La Môme Plage** — Beach club version of La Môme; daytime dining with your feet in the sand

#### Casual / Local Favorites (€15–€30/person)
- **Salama** — 10 Rue Florian; Middle Eastern/Moroccan; hummus, couscous royale, tajines; transforms into a lively nightspot with belly dancers and DJs after dinner; excellent group dinner that flows into a party
- **Al Charq** — 15–17 Rue Rouaze; Lebanese; budget-friendly mezze; excellent vegetarian options; shawarma
- **Tuk Tuk** — 3 Rue Latour-Maubourg; Thai; local "hidden gem"; great value
- **New-York New-York** — 1 Allée de la Liberté; near Palais des Festivals; gourmet burgers, wood-roasted chicken, oversized salads; reliable comfort food

#### Markets & Picnic
- **Marché Forville** — Just off the Old Port; open Tuesday–Sunday, mornings; Cannes' historic covered market with fresh produce, local cheese, olives, charcuterie, socca, and fresh fish; perfect for a self-catered picnic lunch
- **Les Allées de la Liberté** — Weekend outdoor market with vintage goods, plants, flowers, and artisan products

### Dining Tips
- Lunch is the best value meal in France — many restaurants offer a "formule" (set menu) at lunch for €15–€25 (entrée + plat or plat + dessert)
- Dinner reservations are essential during Lions festival week and through July — book ahead online (TheFork / LaFourchette app)
- Service charge is included in French restaurant bills; tipping is optional (round up or leave €2–€5 on the table for good service)
- "Eau du robinet" (tap water) is always free; asking for it avoids the €4–€6/bottle mineral water charge

## Top Attractions

### La Croisette
The iconic palm-lined promenade is Cannes' centerpiece — 3 km of luxury hotels (Carlton, Martinez, Majestic), designer boutiques, beach clubs, and Mediterranean views. Walk it at sunset for classic French Riviera atmosphere. Free; walk it at any time.

### Le Suquet (Old Town)
Cannes' original medieval hilltop village. Climb the narrow cobblestone streets to the **Tour du Suquet (clock tower)** for panoramic views over the bay and Lérins Islands. Stop at **Notre-Dame de l'Espérance church** (17th century). The whole climb takes about 20 minutes; free.

### Palais des Festivals et des Congrès
The modernist venue on the Croisette that hosts both the Film Festival (May) and Lions (June). The red-carpeted steps are a classic photo spot — the handprints of film stars are embedded in plaques nearby. The building is generally accessible for photos when no events are running.

### Marché Forville
Cannes' historic covered market at the foot of Le Suquet; open Tue–Sun, 7 AM–1 PM. The best place to experience local daily life, try socca, buy olive oil, and assemble a picnic. Free entry.

### Lérins Islands (Îles de Lérins)
Two islands a 15–20 minute ferry ride from Quai Laubeuf.

- **Île Sainte-Marguerite:** Fort Royal where the Man in the Iron Mask was imprisoned (fascinating history); turquoise lagoon beaches; eucalyptus forests; excellent snorkeling; free beach access. Ferry: ~€15 round-trip.
- **Île Saint-Honorat:** Ancient functioning Cistercian monastery (est. 410 AD); wine made by monks (tastings available); tranquil and car-free. Entry to monastery museum: €6.50. Ferry: ~€16.50 round-trip if booked online.

**Ferry operators:** Horizon Lérins (horizon-lerins.com) and Riviera Lines; depart from Quai Laubeuf hourly in summer

### Villa Domergue
Hidden Art Deco estate with Italianate gardens; panoramic views; open to public in summer for guided tours. A real hidden gem — excellent photography location.

### Centre d'Art La Malmaison
Small but excellent art center on La Croisette; rotating exhibitions; free or low-cost entry.

### Casino Barrière Les Princes
Open to public (over 18, ID required, smart dress); gaming, restaurant, sophisticated lounge atmosphere. Located near the Palais des Festivals.

## Hidden Gems

- **Rue Meynadier:** A pedestrian street one block behind Rue d'Antibes; local bakeries, cheesemongers, and butchers; where Cannois actually shop
- **Chapelle Bellini:** A 19th-century Italian chapel in a private villa garden; sometimes open to the public; frescoes, olive trees, total calm — 10 minutes from La Croisette
- **Mont Chevalier summit trail:** Walk past Villa Domergue to the very top of Le Suquet hill; a dirt path through pine and olive trees emerges at a viewpoint above the whole city
- **Grasse day trip:** The perfume capital of the world; visit Fragonard or Molinard factory (free tours and samples); the old town is unspoiled; lavender products make great souvenirs; ~40 min by bus
- **Cap d'Antibes loop hike:** The Sentier du Littoral coastal path around Cap d'Antibes (Antibes); 5 km clifftop walk with dramatic sea views; hidden coves for swimming; accessible from Antibes train station

## Shopping & Souvenirs

**Rue d'Antibes:** Cannes' main commercial shopping street, one block inland from La Croisette. High street brands (Zara, Mango), independent French boutiques, and luxury chains. Great for browsing.

**La Croisette boutiques:** High-end designer stores (Chanel, Dior, Louis Vuitton, Bulgari); worth a look even if not buying.

**Marché Forville / Rue Meynadier:** Best for edible souvenirs — local olive oil, tapenade, herbes de Provence, Niçoise confectionery, Grasse-made perfume products.

**Les Allées de la Liberté:** Weekend antique and flower market.

**What to buy:**
- Provençal fabrics and tablecloths (look for "Souleiado" brand)
- Olive oil from the Var region (sold at Forville)
- Rosé wine — pick up a bottle of local Côtes de Provence at a cave à vin
- Lavender products (sachets, essential oils) — authentic ones from Grasse or Provence, not tourist shops
- Perfume from Grasse — visit a factory on a day trip for far better quality and price than airport duty-free

**Bargaining:** Not appropriate in formal shops; acceptable at antique/flea markets

## Day Trips

### 1. Antibes & Juan-les-Pins
- **Distance:** 12 km | **Train:** 8–12 min, ~€3 round-trip
- Visit the charming old town (Vieil Antibes), the Picasso Museum (housed in Château Grimaldi; ~€8), the medieval ramparts, and Port Vauban (one of the Mediterranean's largest yacht harbors)
- Juan-les-Pins: a beach resort with a famous jazz festival in July; excellent beaches; more relaxed than Cannes
- Cap d'Antibes coastal hike: the Sentier du Littoral walk (5 km) offers stunning clifftop views and hidden coves for swimming
- **Best for:** Art & culture, beaches, hiking; easy half-day or full day

### 2. Nice
- **Distance:** 33 km | **Train:** 28–35 min, ~€8–€12 round-trip
- Vieux-Nice (Old Town): colorful Baroque architecture, Cours Saleya market, excellent restaurants
- Promenade des Anglais along the pebble beach
- Castle Hill (Colline du Château): park with panoramic views, waterfall, free entry
- Musée Matisse and Musée Marc Chagall for art lovers
- Great for a full day; significantly bigger than Cannes with excellent dining
- **Best for:** Culture, art, food, a proper city day

### 3. Monaco
- **Distance:** 52 km | **Train:** ~70–80 min (direct or via Nice), ~€14–€18 round-trip
- The Principality: a sovereign microstate; Monte-Carlo Casino (free to enter the lobby; gaming rooms require €10–€17 entrance), Prince's Palace, Oceanographic Museum
- Walk the Prince's Palace hill for views; watch the changing of the guard at 11:55 AM
- The harbor full of superyachts is worth seeing
- Expensive for dining — eat in Cannes or bring snacks
- **Best for:** Spectacle, bucket-list tick; budget 6–8 hours

### 4. Èze Village
- **Distance:** ~45 km | **Train + bus:** ~1 hr; or taxi/Uber from Nice ~30 min
- Medieval hilltop village at 427m; breathtaking views of the coastline
- Exotic garden at the summit (€7): panoramic views; excellent photography
- Narrow stone lanes, perfume boutiques, excellent restaurants
- **Best for:** Photography, romance, scenic views; a half-day with Nice works well together

### 5. Lérins Islands (See Top Attractions above)
- Essentially a half-day beach day with culture attached
- **Best for:** Beach relaxation, snorkeling, history, proposal photography at secluded coves

### 6. Grasse
- **Distance:** 23 km | **Bus from Cannes:** ~40–50 min, ~€2
- Perfume capital of the world; Fragonard, Molinard, and Galimard factories offer free tours and samples
- Charming Old Town with views; excellent market
- Combine with a drive to the Gorges du Loup for hiking and waterfalls
- **Best for:** Cultural day trip; great souvenirs; photography

| **Daily total (per person)** | **~€70–€130** | **~€165–€290** | **€390–€950+** |

**Mid-range benchmarks:**
- Hotel room (mid-range, double): €150–€250/night in June; split between friends
- Dinner for one at a mid-range restaurant: €30–€55 including wine
- Cannes Lions beach pavilion events: mostly free (RSVP) to €20–€50 for ticketed parties
- Ferry to Lérins Islands: €15–€22 round-trip
- Day trip train tickets: €6–€20 round-trip depending on destination
- Beach club day bed: €50–€100/person (minimum spend)
- Club entrance: €0–€40 (Le Baoli); most others free entry with table or at the door

**Tips for mid-range travelers:**
- Eat lunch as your main meal (set menus offer best value)
- Drink rosé by the carafe, not bottle
- Take the TER train for day trips — much cheaper than private transfers
- Picnic at least once — Marché Forville + a bottle of rosé + baguette and cheese = perfection

## Apps & Connectivity

- **SIM card:** Buy a French/EU SIM at Nice Airport or a Fnac/Darty store (Orange, SFR, Free Mobile); ~€10–€20 for a monthly data SIM with EU roaming. Alternatively use an eSIM (Airalo, Holafly) — most cost-effective
- **Wi-Fi:** Available in most hotels, cafés, restaurants; reliable quality in Cannes
- **Useful apps:**
  - SNCF Connect / Trainline: train tickets
  - Uber / Bolt: ride-hailing
  - TheFork (LaFourchette): restaurant reservations
  - Google Maps / Maps.me: navigation (download offline map of French Riviera)
  - Revolut / Wise: fee-free currency exchange and overseas spending
  - Google Translate: camera translation for French menus
